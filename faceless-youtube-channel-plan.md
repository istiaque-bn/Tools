# Faceless YouTube Channel Plan

*Prepared July 2026*

## The rules you're playing under

To earn ad revenue you need the YouTube Partner Program: **1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours in 12 months** (long-form), or 1,000 subscribers + 10M Shorts views in 90 days. You also need an approved AdSense account and no strikes.

AI/faceless content **is** monetizable, but with a hard catch: YouTube's inauthentic-content policy demonetizes mass-produced, repetitious content (verbatim text-to-speech over stock slideshows). Enforcement is a three-strike system ending in permanent YPP removal. What survives review: your own scripts and perspective, custom editing and pacing, and the "altered/synthetic content" disclosure toggled when AI visuals are used. AI as a production tool is fine; AI as the whole channel is not.

Extra caution zones: financial/medical advice framed as advice, real people depicted without consent, misleading current-events content.

## Niche options (RPM = revenue per 1,000 monetized views)

| Niche | RPM | Effort | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Personal finance / credit | $25–45 | High | Highest-paying on YouTube; crowded; needs accuracy and "not financial advice" framing |
| Investing / markets | $18–35 | High | Chart animations, portfolio breakdowns; competitive |
| AI tools / make money online | $10–25 | Medium | Fast-moving, constant fresh topics; screen recordings work well |
| Business/company documentaries | $8–18 | Medium | "How X went bankrupt" style; evergreen, story-driven — good fit for strong scripts |
| True crime / animated horror | $6–13 | Medium | Reliable demand; heavier research + sensitivity care |

**Recommendation: business/company documentaries or AI tools.** Both reward good writing (which I can help with heavily), don't require on-camera presence or advice disclaimers, and have evergreen or perpetually fresh topic supply. Finance pays more but is saturated and higher-risk under advice policies.

## Production workflow (per video)

1. **Topic research** — I find an underserved angle with search demand
2. **Script** — 1,200–1,800 words (~8–12 min video), written with a distinct voice, not AI filler
3. **Voiceover** — best: record it yourself (strongest originality signal). Alternative: ElevenLabs with heavy customization
4. **Visuals** — screen recordings, licensed b-roll (Storyblocks/Pexels), simple motion graphics; avoid generic stock slideshows
5. **Edit** — CapCut or DaVinci Resolve (both free); cut for pacing, add captions
6. **Thumbnail + title** — I draft 3 title options + thumbnail concept per video
7. **Upload** — toggle synthetic-content disclosure if AI visuals used

Target cadence: **1–2 long-form videos/week**. Quality beats volume under 2026 policy.

## Monetization roadmap

- **Months 1–3:** 15–25 videos published. Expect near-zero revenue. Goal: find which formats get click-through and retention; double down.
- **Months 3–6:** If a format hits, watch hours compound. Many channels cross 1,000 subs here; 4,000 hours is usually the slower gate.
- **Month 6+:** Apply to YPP. Before ads, monetize via **affiliate links** (works from day one — tool niches especially), then sponsorships once views justify it.

Realistic expectation: most faceless channels fail from quitting before 25 videos or from copying saturated formats. Plan for 6 months before meaningful ad revenue.
